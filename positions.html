Open positions. If you are interested in the application of mathematical and computational methods in biology, we plan to admit new graduate students (PhD or MSc), starting in september 2014, to work on computational paleogenomics problems.
Candidates with a BSc in mathematics or computer science and a serious interest in computational biology are welcome to apply either through the Department of Mathematics, SFU (deadline January 15th, 2014) or the MADD-GEN graduate program (Computational Methods for the Analysis of the Dynamics and Diversity of Genomes).
Alternatively, you can apply to the School of Computing Science, SFU (deadline February 1st, 2014) or the CIHR/MSFHR bioinformatics training program.
Finally, we welcome at any time applications from motivated undergraduate students to work with us, either as coop interns or as USRA interns.
